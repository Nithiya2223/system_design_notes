# Behavioral Questions for Engineers

## 🧭 Overview
Technical skill gets you in the door, but **behavioral interviews** often decide the outcome — especially at companies like Amazon (Leadership Principles) where they carry major weight. This guide covers the STAR method, common question themes, and how to prepare authentic, structured stories that demonstrate impact and ownership.

---

## 🧠 The STAR Method
Structure every answer as:
- **S — Situation:** brief context (what, when, your role).
- **T — Task:** the challenge/goal you owned.
- **A — Action:** what *you* specifically did (use "I," not "we").
- **R — Result:** the outcome, ideally quantified ("reduced latency 40%," "saved $X").

Keep it ~2 minutes. Lead with the point, then support it. Emphasize **your** contribution and what you **learned**.

---

## 🗂️ Common Question Themes (with sample prompts)

### Ownership & Impact
- "Tell me about a project you drove end to end."
- "Describe a time you went beyond your role."

### Conflict & Collaboration
- "Tell me about a disagreement with a teammate/manager. How did you resolve it?"
- "Describe working with a difficult stakeholder."

### Failure & Learning
- "Tell me about a time you failed or made a significant mistake."
- "A time you received tough feedback — what did you do?"

### Dealing with Ambiguity
- "A time you had to make a decision with incomplete information."
- "How did you handle shifting requirements?"

### Prioritization & Deadlines
- "A time you had to meet a tight deadline / juggle competing priorities."
- "How did you handle being overcommitted?"

### Leadership & Influence
- "A time you influenced a decision without authority."
- "How did you mentor someone?"

### Technical Judgment
- "A difficult technical trade-off you made and why."
- "A time you disagreed with a technical approach."

---

## ✅ Preparation Strategy
1. **Build a story bank:** prepare 8–12 stories from real experience covering the themes above. One strong story can answer several prompts.
2. **Map stories to company values** (e.g., Amazon's Leadership Principles: Ownership, Customer Obsession, Dive Deep, Bias for Action, Disagree and Commit).
3. **Quantify results** wherever possible.
4. **Practice out loud** and time yourself (~2 min).
5. **Be honest** — interviewers probe; fabricated stories collapse under follow-ups.

---

## ⚖️ Do / Don't

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Use "I" to claim your actions | Hide behind "we" the whole time |
| Pick real, specific stories | Give vague or hypothetical answers |
| Quantify the result | End with "and it worked out" |
| Show learning/growth from failure | Blame others or deny mistakes |
| Keep it ~2 minutes | Ramble for 8 minutes |
| Tailor to the company's values | Use one generic answer for everything |

---

## 🍎 Simple Tip
Think of behavioral answers like a good news headline: state the impactful result early, then explain how you got there. The interviewer should never have to ask "...so what happened?"

---

## 🎯 Sample Questions with Approach Hints
1. [Amazon] "Tell me about a time you had to dive deep to solve a problem." → **Hint:** Show data-driven root-cause analysis and the concrete fix (Dive Deep).
2. [Amazon] "A time you disagreed with your manager." → **Hint:** Disagree respectfully with data, then commit to the decision (Have Backbone; Disagree and Commit).
3. [Meta] "Tell me about your most impactful project." → **Hint:** Scope, your role, measurable impact, and what you'd do differently.
4. [Google] "A time you handled ambiguity." → **Hint:** How you scoped the problem, made assumptions, and iterated.
5. [Netflix] "A time you took a big risk or made a tough call." → **Hint:** Judgment, ownership, and the outcome/learning.
6. [General] "Your biggest failure." → **Hint:** Own it honestly, focus on what you learned and changed afterward.
